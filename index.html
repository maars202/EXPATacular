Fun Facts about Singapore!

Made up of 21 Islands

Singapore’s land area includes as many as 64 offshore islands that surround the main island.

World's First Night Zoo

Singapore’s Night Safari provides a nocturnal experience like no other

Booking a table with tissue

locals use tissue packs to make "table reservations" at hawker centres

World's First F1 Night Race

Held annually since 2008, Grand Prix Season Singapore is the only F1 Race held in the night!

Military Service is Mandatory for all Singaporean Men

Every Singaporean Male serves the nation for two years after finishing high school and returns to train till the age of 40!

Bukit Timah Hill is Singapore's tallest natural point

The tallest natural point in Singapore, Bukit Timah Hill, is only 164 meters high.

Welcome to your new home!

Singapore is home to over 2.16 Million Immigrants

That's over 40% of our total population

Throughout its history as a port city, Singapore has been a melting pot of cultures and nationalities. Currently, as one of Asia’s most important economies, it attracts people from all over the world and of different socioeconomic backgrounds to seek better opportunities. The immigrant population is largely of Asian origin, with neighboring Malaysians making up the majority.

Lacking in natural resources, Singapore’s government has long stressed that its most important resource is its people. However, with declining birth rates and low population growth, the country increasingly relies on immigrants to help shoulder the economic burden.
